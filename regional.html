Regional cuisine

Cuisine differs across India's diverse regions as a result of variation in local culture, geographical location (proximity to sea, desert, or mountains), and economics. It also varies seasonally, depending on which fruits and vegetables are ripe.

Regions Main State Names

West India Maharashtra, Gujarat, and Goa.
South India Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, Kerala and Telangana

Northern indian food

Perhaps the most prevalent culinary style found outside of India, Northern Indian cuisine reflects a strong Mughal influence. It is characterized by a high use of dairy: milk, paneer (an Indian mild cheese), ghee (clarified butter), and yogurt are all used regularly in Northern dishes. Samosas, fried pastries stuffed with potatoes and occasionally meat, are a distinctive Northern snack. Clay ovens known as tandoors are popular in the North, giving dishes like Tandoori Chicken and Naan bread their distinctive charcoal flavor. A significant number of Northern dishes make regular appearances on Indian menus. Dal or Paneer Makhani are popular vegetarian dishes, consisting of dal or paneer cooked in a creamy sauce of tomatoes, onions, mango powder, and garam masala. Saag Paneer and Palak Paneer are two similar dishes made with spinach, cream, and paneer, differing slightly in consistency and spices. Korma, another menu staple from Northern India, is a creamy curry of coconut milk or yogurt, cumin, coriander, and small amounts of cashews or almonds. It can be served with different meats, usually chicken or lamb, but sometimes beef, as well as with paneer for a vegetarian dish.

Jammu and Kashmir Cuisine

The northernmost state of India brings forth one of the most delicious cuisines to be found anywhere in the country. Kashmir is known as the ‘Heaven on Earth’ due its gorgeous lakes, mountains, and valleys. It is also heavenly because of its cuisine. The cuisine of Jammu and Kashmir is divided into two categories. There is a difference between Jammu cuisine which is largely vegetarian and Kashmiri cuisine which is largely influenced by the Kashmiri Pandit cuisine and the Waza cuisine. The Wazas mainly specializes in a multi-course meal called Wazwan.

Rogan Josh is one of the most popular dishes of the Kashmir Valley. If you are a lamb or mutton lover, you will surely love this dish. It is made up of lamb, yogurt, aromatic spices, and caramelized onions. The Mughals have introduced this mouthwatering dish to India. People who love meat but hate to gain the calories would simply fall in love with this dish. One can eat it with rice or naan or even both. The dum aloo, yakhni, haak saag, gustaba and tabak maaz are some of the well-liked dishes served in this region. One incredible delicacy to taste is the kalaadi cheese, which is a traditional local hill cheese (usually made from cow’s milk) native to this land

Punjabi Cuisine

Punjabi cuisine is a culinary style originating in the Punjab, a region in the northern part of the Indian subcontinent, which is now divided in an Indian part and a Pakistani part. This cuisine has a rich tradition of many distinct and local ways of cooking. One is a special form of tandoori cooking that is now famous in other parts of India, UK, Canada, Hong Kong and in many parts of the world.

This northern state is especially popular for its amazing local cuisine that is relished throughout the country.The Makke ki roti and sarson da saag, which is a popular combination of a Punjabi flat-bread and gravy made of mustard leaves and spices. Also, the popular chhola-bhatura, rajma-chawal, amritsari machhli (fish) and Lassi (sweetened buttermilk) find their roots in this region.

Haryana Cuisine

Haryana is one of the wealthiest states in India with humble and kindhearted people. The occupation of the majority is agriculture and dairy. Haryanvi's diet is a vegetarian one, and they consume a lot of milk and ghee. In fact, a lot of people make butter and ghee at home. Haryana is also referred to as 'The Land of Rotis".

Kadhi is the gravy made from sour yoghurt and is thickened using gram flour. What makes the recipe unique in Haryana and Punjab is that the folks add fried pakoras (fritters) to the Kadhi making it perfect Indian comfort food. Malpua is an Indian version of sweet pancakes. They are fluffy and are served hot. Malpua is made and garnished in ghee. In some places, malpua is served with rabri (a condensed-milk-based sweet dish).
